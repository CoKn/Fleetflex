## Figma Make Prompt: FleetFlex Landing Page

Create a modern B2B landing page for a sustainability-focused energy platform called **FleetFlex**.

The page should help an airport, energy company, or corporate fleet operator quickly understand the concept.

## Core concept

**FleetFlex helps airports turn their electric vehicle fleets into green energy buffers.**

Frankfurt Airport is already moving toward renewable electricity. The problem is not only getting green energy — the problem is using it at the right time.

FleetFlex stores renewable-backed electricity in parked airport EV batteries, uses it first to power airport operations, and sells only verified surplus green flexibility to Mainova or another energy-market partner.

Core logic:

**Protect vehicles → Power airport → Sell surplus to Mainova**

---

# Landing page structure

## 1. Hero section

Headline:

**Turn airport EV fleets into green energy buffers**

Subheadline:

**FleetFlex helps airports store renewable electricity in parked EVs, use it when airport demand peaks, and sell verified surplus green flexibility to the market.**

Primary CTA:

**Explore the platform**

Secondary CTA:

**View sustainability impact**

Add a clean hero visual:

* airport terminal silhouette
* EV fleet charging
* battery storage icon
* renewable energy flow
* energy marketplace connection to Mainova

Use a professional enterprise SaaS style: clean, trustworthy, sustainability-focused, not too playful.

---

## 2. Pain points section

Section headline:

**Airports are under pressure to prove real climate action**

Use 4 cards:

### Card 1: Aviation reputation pressure

Flying is visibly linked to CO₂ emissions, creating public and marketing pressure for airports.

### Card 2: Renewable energy is already growing

Frankfurt Airport is already moving strongly toward renewable electricity, aiming for almost fully renewable electricity sourcing.

### Card 3: The real challenge is timing

Solar peaks during the day, wind fluctuates, and airport demand continues at night and during operational peaks.

### Card 4: EV batteries are underused

Airport EV batteries are already on-site, but without optimization they are mostly used only for mobility, not as flexible green energy storage.

Add one highlighted quote-style statement:

**The question is no longer only “How do airports get green electricity?”
It is “How do airports make green electricity usable when it is needed most?”**

---

## 3. Scalability section

Section headline:

**This is not only a Frankfurt Airport problem**

Use a map-inspired or network visual of Germany.

Bullet points:

* Germany has over 100 airports facing similar sustainability and energy challenges.
* Airports have high electricity demand, growing EV fleets, and increasing pressure to decarbonize.
* The same model can scale to logistics depots, municipal fleets, delivery fleets, and corporate campuses.
* Large EV fleets can become distributed renewable energy buffers.

Add metric cards:

* **103 airports in Germany**
* **Growing EV fleets**
* **High energy demand**
* **Need for flexible storage**

---

## 4. Solution section

Section headline:

**FleetFlex makes renewable airport energy dispatchable**

Show a 3-step horizontal flow:

### Step 1: Protect vehicles

FleetFlex checks vehicle schedules, battery state, charger status, and required state of charge.

### Step 2: Power airport

The platform discharges selected EVs into airport demand during expensive or high-demand periods.

### Step 3: Sell surplus

Only after airport operations are secured, verified surplus green flexibility is sold to Mainova or another market partner.

Add a visual energy flow:

**Renewable electricity → EV batteries → Airport operations → Surplus marketplace → Mainova**

Add supporting text:

**FleetFlex does not replace Fraport’s renewable strategy. It makes that strategy more valuable by turning renewable electricity into flexible, storable, and marketable energy.**

---

## 5. Platform features section

Section headline:

**One platform for green energy optimization**

Use 6 feature cards:

### Renewable charging optimizer

Charges vehicles when renewable-backed electricity is available or cheap.

### Vehicle readiness protection

Prevents discharge if a vehicle is needed for airport operations.

### Airport energy buffer

Uses EV batteries to power airport demand during peak periods.

### Surplus detector

Identifies only the remaining verified surplus after airport needs are secured.

### Mainova marketplace interface

Packages surplus capacity into structured green flexibility blocks for Mainova or another energy-market partner.

### Impact dashboard

Tracks cost savings, surplus revenue, battery usage, and sustainability impact.

---

## 6. Impact section

Section headline:

**Why airports use FleetFlex**

Create 5 impact cards:

### Lower energy costs

Charge during cheap renewable-heavy periods and reduce grid purchases during expensive peaks.

### New revenue stream

Sell verified surplus green flexibility after airport needs are covered.

### Better use of existing assets

EV batteries become both mobility assets and energy storage assets.

### Stronger sustainability story

The airport is not only buying green energy — it is storing, using, and monetizing it.

### Better public image

Position the airport as a green-energy infrastructure player, not only a high-emission transport hub.

Add a small dashboard mockup with KPIs:

* Energy cost savings
* Surplus revenue
* Renewable energy stored
* CO₂ impact estimate
* Vehicle readiness violations: 0

---

## 7. Mainova section

Section headline:

**Why Mainova buys the surplus**

Explain Mainova’s role clearly:

**Mainova is the energy-market partner. FleetFlex does not ask Mainova to manage individual vehicles. Instead, FleetFlex packages surplus battery capacity into clean, structured flexibility offers.**

Example offer card:

**Surplus offer to Mainova**

* Available energy: **420 kWh**
* Delivery window: **20:00–22:00**
* Source: **Renewable-backed airport EV storage**
* Airport demand protected: **Yes**
* Vehicle readiness protected: **Yes**
* Status: **Ready for market**

Then add bullets:

* Mainova gets access to local renewable-backed flexibility.
* Mainova can use it for portfolio optimization.
* Mainova can support green-energy products.
* Mainova can use it for intraday trading or balancing its energy position.
* Mainova avoids dealing with unmanaged individual EVs.

## 8. Go-to-market section

Section headline:

**Start with Frankfurt Airport, then scale**

Use a 4-phase roadmap:

### Phase 1: Co-create with Fraport

Start with one depot or one operational vehicle group at Frankfurt Airport.

Optimize renewable charging, vehicle readiness, airport-side discharging, and surplus detection.

### Phase 3: Add Mainova marketplace layer

Sell verified surplus green flexibility to Mainova or another energy-market partner.

### Phase 4: Scale to other fleets

Expand to other airports, logistics depots, municipal fleets, and corporate EV campuses.

Add KPI targets:

* Energy cost savings
* Surplus revenue
* Renewable energy utilization
* CO₂ impact
* Vehicle readiness reliability

# Design direction

Use a premium B2B SaaS landing page style.

Visual style:

* white and very light gray background
* dark navy text
* green and electric blue accents
* clean cards with rounded corners
* subtle shadows
* energy-flow diagrams
* airport and EV illustrations
* professional charts and KPI widgets
* sustainability but not “greenwashing”

Tone:

* credible
* strategic
* sustainability-focused
* enterprise-ready
* clean and confident

Avoid:

* consumer-app feel
* overly playful illustrations
* vague climate slogans
* claiming that all battery energy is automatically green

Key message to repeat visually:

**Protect vehicles → Power airport → Sell surplus**
